Historical Origins

Lunar New Year is one of the most important and meaningful festivals in many Asian countries, especially in China, Vietnam, Korea, and Southeast Asia. It marks the beginning of the new year according to the lunar calendar and usually takes place in late January or early February.

The history of Lunar New Year goes back thousands of years. People believed the new year was a chance to say goodbye to bad luck and welcome good fortune. Farmers celebrated the end of the farming year and hoped for good weather and a successful harvest.

Tet Holiday in Vietnam

Tet Holiday is the most significant traditional celebration in Vietnam. Several weeks before Tet, families clean and decorate their homes beautifully. Preparing for Tet is not only about food and decorations but also about spiritual values.

Traditional dishes such as Banh Chung and Banh Tet are prepared as symbols of gratitude and family unity.

Tet is a time for people to reflect on the past year, express gratitude to ancestors, and set positive goals for the future.

Important Traditions

Red Envelopes (Hongbao)

Red envelopes symbolize good fortune and prosperity. Elders give them to younger family members. The red color represents happiness and protection from evil spirits.

Reunion Dinner

The Reunion Dinner takes place on New Year's Eve. Families gather together to share food, show gratitude, and welcome a prosperous new year.

Lion Dance and Dragon Dance

Lion and dragon dances are vibrant performances believed to bring good luck and drive away evil spirits. They are accompanied by drums and cymbals.

Firecrackers and Fireworks

Firecrackers and fireworks are used to scare away evil spirits and negative energy. This tradition comes from the legend of the monster Nian.

New Year Markets and Fairs

Markets before Lunar New Year are full of decorations, traditional foods, street games, and cultural performances.

Global Celebrations

Lunar New Year is celebrated worldwide. In China, there are grand parades and fireworks. In Vietnam, people celebrate Tet Nguyen Dan and enjoy traditional foods like banh chung.
